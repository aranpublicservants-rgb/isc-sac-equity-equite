Overview

A summary of the published figures. Each section gives the source. Where a figure is worked out from official numbers, it is marked computed.

Published figures at a glance

  • Representation, March 31, 2024: Indigenous Peoples 27.4% (availability 5.7%); women 70.0% (66.3%); racialized employees 20.1% (22.8%); persons with disabilities 8.1% (12.0%).
  • ISC's Indigenous representation of 27.4% is the highest among federal departments in the core public administration. The next highest is Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada at 17.6%.
  • Two groups are below ISC's workforce availability: persons with disabilities (about 320 fewer than the availability figure) and racialized employees (about 227 fewer).
  • 2024 employee survey, ISC compared with the public service: harassment 16% (public service 12%); discrimination 10% (9%); can use formal recourse without fear of reprisal 46% (51%); racism has harmed my mental health 23% (16%).
  • Some data is not published at the department level, including representation by executive level, the First Nations, Inuit and Métis breakdown, acting appointments, and time to provide an accommodation. These are listed in Findings & data gaps and can be requested in Request the data.

How to read the figures

  • A bar with a marker. The bar is ISC's representation. The gold marker is ISC's workforce availability. When the bar is shorter than the marker, representation is below availability. The text beside each bar states this in words.
  • Gaps in people and in points. Both are shown, because a point difference and a headcount difference can each matter for different purposes.
  • Source labels. official taken from TBS, PSC or ISC. computed worked out from official figures. enter a value you supply.
  • "supp." means suppressed. Counts of 1 to 5 are withheld to protect privacy. Suppressed does not mean zero.

Notes on the data

  • Workforce availability is the benchmark under the Employment Equity Act. A figure below availability is the trigger for a barrier review under the Act. It is not a finding of non-compliance.
  • Representation counts only employees who choose to self-identify, so reported figures may be lower than the actual share.
  • ISC's workforce availability differs from the public service figure because availability is based on ISC's occupations and regions.

Indigenous workforce

ISC reports Indigenous representation as a single figure (27.4%). The breakdowns that the figure does not show are listed here, with where to find them.

Breakdowns not in the annual table

  • By identity. The split into First Nations, Inuit, Métis and other Indigenous identities is published public-service-wide in the snapshot, and by department in the PSC dashboard.
  • By level. Representation by classification group and executive level, by Indigenous identity, is not published at the department level.
  • Acting appointments. Acting appointments by Indigenous identity are in the PSC dashboard, not the annual table.

Where to find the breakdown

The PSC employment equity by organization dashboard reports employees, applicants, hires, promotions and acting appointments by Indigenous identity for each department. The request generator builds an internal request for the breakdown by level and length of service.

2023 to 2024 internal consultation

  • 25.8% reported experiencing attitudinal barriers at work; 17.0% reported observing a co-worker experience them.
  • Accommodation results over two years: 50% received the accommodation, 5.0% were refused, 37.5% were in progress, 7.5% received a measure other than the one requested.
  • 27.9% felt well supported by IT with accessible software; 8.2% did not feel supported and did not know who to ask.
  • 84.1% found virtual meetings fully accessible.

ISC and IOGC 2023 to 2025 Accessibility Plan, internal employee consultation. official

Better Accommodation Project audit

ISC took part in an interdepartmental accommodation project and ran an external audit. The published findings record that employees described the process as complicated, focused on cost, and slow, with some delays lasting years and documentation requirements placed on the employee. Two Indigenous Talking Circles, led by an Elder, addressed barriers for Indigenous employees with disabilities. ISC states it is redesigning the duty-to-accommodate process around the social model of disability, with a "yes by default" approach for low-cost accommodations.

ISC and IOGC 2026 to 2028 Accessibility Plan, December 2025. 256 employees with disabilities and 90 managers surveyed. official

Data not published

The time from an accommodation request to the accommodation being in place is not published. This figure, by year and with disability separations alongside, would show whether the redesign changes the outcome. It can be requested in Request the data (see also Findings & data gaps, item B2).

Findings and data gaps

Each item states a finding from published data, the federal laws, regulations and directives that apply to it, and the related data that is not published at the department level. Under the Employment Equity Act, representation below workforce availability is the trigger to identify and remove employment barriers. These are items to investigate. They are not findings of non-compliance. You can copy any item, or all the ones shown, as a note.

Context

Facts about ISC that affect how to read the data.

Department

ISC was created by Order in Council in November 2017 and established by the Department of Indigenous Services Act on July 15, 2019, from the former Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development. Its mandate is to support First Nations, Inuit and Métis in taking control of services. ISC's 2024 to 2025 Departmental Results Report states that its mandate includes addressing systemic racism and discrimination rooted in colonialism, and refers to Joyce's Principle.

ISC Info Source; ISC 2024 to 2025 Departmental Results Report. official

Service delivery and workforce availability

Most ISC services are delivered through funding agreements and directly in the regions and the North, including primary care and public-health nursing, Jordan's Principle, the Inuit Child First Initiative, status registration and environmental public health. ISC's workforce availability for Indigenous Peoples (5.7%) is higher than the public service figure because availability reflects ISC's occupations and regions.

Human resources and restructuring

  • 2024: ISC established its own HR service, ending the shared model with CIRNAC that operated from 2019.
  • February 2025: a sustainability review began.
  • September 2025: a restructuring toward a single-window regional model and an expanded digital approach for individual clients.

Separation figures during this period include workforce-adjustment departures, resignations and retirements, which the published totals do not separate. See Findings & data gaps, item B7.

ISC 2026 to 2028 Accessibility Plan. official

Sources, limits and accessibility

Every figure is taken from a published Government of Canada source. Computed values are worked out from official figures. No figure is estimated or modelled.

Definitions

Workforce availabilityThe estimated share of a designated group in the Canadian workforce who could be recruited into the relevant jobs, based on the 2021 Census and the 2022 Canadian Survey on Disability. This tool uses ISC's departmental availability, which reflects ISC's occupations and regions. It is the benchmark under the Employment Equity Act.
RepresentationThe share of employees who self-identify with a group. Self-identification is voluntary, so the actual share may be higher than reported. Sex is taken from the pay system.
Expected number and differenceExpected = headcount × workforce availability. Difference = actual minus expected, shown in people and in percentage points.
Positive answers (survey)The survey's total of favourable answers for a question, after setting aside "don't know" and "not applicable." For "victim of harassment" and "victim of discrimination," the figure shown is the share who answered yes.
Suppressed (supp.)Counts of 1 to 5 are withheld to protect privacy. Suppressed does not mean zero.

Limits

  • Representation is the March 31, 2024 cycle, the most recent published at the department level with a workforce availability figure. ISC's 2024 to 2025 department row appears in the Demographic Snapshot Appendix Table 1, linked below.
  • Representation by executive level, and by Indigenous identity at the executive level, is not published by department.
  • The disability availability figure (12.0%) uses the broader 2017 survey definition, which the current self-identification form does not match, so part of the difference reflects measurement.
  • Survey results shown here are ISC-wide. The breakdown by group within ISC is in the PSES data tools.
  • Separation figures include retirements and workforce-adjustment departures, which are not separated in the published totals.

Writing, terminology and accessibility

This page follows federal guidance on plain language, terminology and accessibility, including the Canada.ca Content Style Guide, the Translation Bureau's inclusive-writing guidance and its terminology resources on Indigenous Peoples (for example the Words First guide and Termium Plus), the Standard on Web Accessibility, the Guideline on Making Communications Products and Activities Accessible, and WCAG 2.1 AA. It uses "Indigenous Peoples" and names First Nations, Inuit and Métis distinctly where the data allows. It uses "racialized employees" (data label: members of visible minorities) and "persons with disabilities." It states designated-group standing in words and symbols, not by colour alone. Tables use row and column headers and captions. The tabs follow the standard keyboard pattern. The generator and calculator announce their results to screen readers.
